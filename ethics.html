Ethics Page

Why Ethics Matters in Information Technology

Technology affects privacy, safety, fairness, accountability, and public trust. Because of that, IT professionals, software developers, and data scientists must think beyond technical performance and consider the impact of their work on people.

Guidelines for Developers

Developers should build systems that reduce harm, protect users, and support the public good. The ACM Code of Ethics emphasizes contributing to society and human well-being, avoiding harm, being honest and trustworthy, respecting privacy, and honoring confidentiality. These ideas matter when developers design applications, collect user data, patch vulnerabilities, and respond to incidents.

Ethical Considerations for Data Scientists and AI Teams

Data scientists and AI practitioners must think carefully about bias, transparency, explainability, and risk. The NIST AI Risk Management Framework explains that AI risk management should address impacts on individuals, organizations, and society. In practice, this means teams should validate data sources, test for harmful bias, document limitations, and monitor systems after deployment.

Responsible Professional Conduct

The Software Engineering Code of Ethics stresses that software engineers should act consistently with the public interest, the interests of clients and employers, and the integrity of the profession. Ethical practice includes reporting known risks, refusing deceptive shortcuts, and maintaining quality and security standards even under pressure.

My View

In IT, technical skill alone is not enough. Strong systems must also be trustworthy. Whether someone works in networking, software, cybersecurity, or AI, ethics should be part of design, deployment, maintenance, and decision-making from the start.
